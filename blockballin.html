My Contributions

Inspired by games such as Cut the Rope and Where's my Water?, I worked with 3 teammates to create a challenging puzzle-platformer with hand-crafted levels across 4 weeks.

We focused on crafting levels that allowed players to explore all the different affordances of one main mechanic rather than introducing new mechanics. We prioritized creating open solution spaces to maximize player creativity and create meaningful choices within a small space.

Balancing dexterity requirements with slow-and-steady strategy proved to be a large challenge. Using embedded hints and gates, we provided many moments of pause for players to strategize, while also posing reaction time challenges in other moments.

During development I acted as the team's producer, redirecting the game's direction every week based on playtest notes and delegating tasks to all team members.

Hand-crafted 26 unique and quirky puzzles

Crafted 26 levels with sinewave level progression and a focus on open solution spaces. Levels have varied focus on dexterity vs strategy with embedded hints and gates.

Prefabbed blocks and level pieces

Created prefabs of all the block and various level pieces to expedite level design process & iteration for all team members.

Tutorialized block affordances

Mapped out block affordances in a level design document and constructed levels to tutorialize those affordances.

Regular playtesting with varied groups

Playtested regularly with different player groups of different experiences & ages across 4 weeks to test the game's difficulty progression.
